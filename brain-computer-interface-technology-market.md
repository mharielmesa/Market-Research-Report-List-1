<p><p>Brain Computer Interface (BCI) technology allows a direct connection between the human brain and a computer system. The market for BCI technology can be categorized into two types: invasive BCI and non-invasive BCI. Invasive BCI requires the implantation of sensors or electrodes into the brain tissue, which can provide more precise and accurate readings but poses potential risks. On the other hand, non-invasive BCI relies on external devices like headsets or caps to capture brain signals without the need for surgery, ensuring convenience and safety for users.</p></p>
