<p><p>A radial miniature probe is a medical device that is used for intravascular ultrasound (IVUS) procedures. It consists of a tiny ultrasound transducer mounted on the tip of a catheter, which allows doctors to visualize and evaluate the blood vessels from inside. The probe is inserted into the patient's blood vessels, providing real-time images and measurements of the blood vessel walls, plaque deposits, and other abnormalities.</p><p>The global radial miniature probe market is experiencing steady growth and is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.1% during the forecast period. <p><p>The radial miniature probe market includes two types based on ultrasonic frequency: 12MHz and 20MHz. The 12MHz variant utilizes ultrasonic waves at this frequency for medical imaging purposes. It provides a good balance between penetration depth and resolution, making it suitable for various applications such as cardiac and vascular imaging. On the other hand, the 20MHz probe operates at a higher ultrasonic frequency. While it offers superior resolution, it is less suitable for deep tissue imaging and is commonly used for specialized applications like ophthalmology or superficial imaging of skin and soft tissues.</p></p>
