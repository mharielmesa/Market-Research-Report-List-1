<p><p>Enteral access devices refer to medical equipment used to deliver nutrition directly into the stomach or intestines when a patient cannot consume food orally. There are several types of enteral access devices available in the market. Enteral feeding tubes are flexible tubes inserted through the nose, mouth, or abdomen to reach the digestive tract. Enteral feeding pumps are electronic devices that control the rate at which the liquid nutrition is administered. Administration sets are tubing systems used to connect feeding tubes to the pumping devices. Enteral syringes are used to deliver medicine or liquid nutrition in small amounts. Other types of enteral access devices include gastric access devices and jejunal tubes.</p></p>
