<p><p>The medical exoskeleton market consists of two main types: metal and plastic. Metal exoskeletons are made from materials such as titanium or aluminum, offering durability and strength. These exoskeletons are commonly used in rehabilitation settings to aid patients with mobility impairments. On the other hand, plastic exoskeletons are made from lightweight materials like polyethylene or polypropylene, providing flexibility and comfort. Plastic exoskeletons are often used in situations requiring prolonged wear, such as for individuals with spinal cord injuries or muscular dystrophy. Both metal and plastic exoskeletons play a vital role in assisting individuals with physical challenges to regain mobility and improve their quality of life.</p></p>
