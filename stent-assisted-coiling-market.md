<p><p>Stent-assisted coiling is a minimally invasive endovascular procedure used to treat intracranial aneurysms. It involves the placement of a stent within the affected blood vessel to provide support and stability, followed by the insertion of platinum coils to promote clotting and prevent rupture. This technique offers several advantages over traditional coiling procedures, including improved aneurysm occlusion rates, reduced risk of recanalization, and enhanced long-term patient outcomes.</p><p>The global stent-assisted coiling market is experiencing significant growth, driven by various factors. Firstly, there is an increasing prevalence of intracranial aneurysms worldwide, primarily due to factors such as aging population, lifestyle changes, and higher diagnostic rates. Different types of stent-assisted coiling market include self-expandable stents that can press against vessel walls without external force, liquid embolic devices which fill the aneurysm with a special glue-like substance, flow-diversion devices that redirect blood flow away from the aneurysm, and embolic coils that are metallic devices placed inside the aneurysm to promote blood clotting and prevent further rupture.</p></p>
