<p><p>Pharmaceutical drug delivery refers to the methods used for administering medications to patients. There are various market types in this field, including oral, pulmonary, injectable, and topical delivery. Oral drug delivery involves ingesting medications through the mouth, allowing them to be absorbed through the digestive system. Pulmonary delivery delivers drugs directly to the lungs through inhalation. Injectable delivery involves administering medications through injections into a patient's bloodstream or tissues. Topical delivery involves applying medications directly onto the skin or mucous membranes for local or systemic effects. Each market type offers unique advantages and is tailored to specific patient needs.</p></p>
