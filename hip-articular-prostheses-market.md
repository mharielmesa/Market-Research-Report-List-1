<p><p>Hip articular prostheses are medical devices used in hip replacement surgeries to enhance mobility and alleviate pain. The market for these prostheses can be divided into cemented and non-cemented fixation types. Cemented fixation involves using a type of bone cement to secure the prosthesis to the existing bone, providing immediate stability. In contrast, non-cemented fixation relies on the natural bone growth to create a stable union between the prosthesis and bone over time. Both fixation types have their advantages and considerations, and the choice depends on factors such as patient age, bone quality, and surgeon preference.</p></p>
