<p><p>Hepcidin is a peptide hormone produced in the liver that regulates iron metabolism in the body. It plays a crucial role in iron homeostasis by controlling the absorption of iron from the diet and the release of iron from cells. Hepcidin acts by binding to and degrading ferroportin, a protein responsible for exporting iron from cells into the bloodstream.</p><p>The Hepcidin Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by factors such as an increasing prevalence of iron-related disorders, growing awareness about the importance of iron regulation, and advancements in diagnostic tools for measuring hepcidin levels. Iron disorders, including iron deficiency anemia, hereditary hemochromatosis, and anemia of chronic disease, are increasingly being recognized as critical health concerns worldwide. This has led to a rise in the demand for hepcidin-based therapies and diagnostic tests.</p><p>Moreover, the rising geriatric population, who are more susceptible to iron disorders, and the growing number of blood disorders are further fueling the market growth.
