<p><p>The bioengineered protein drugs market comprises a variety of product types. Recombinant protein drugs are artificially produced proteins used to treat various diseases. Peptide hormones refer to protein-based hormones used to regulate physiological processes. Vaccines are bioengineered to stimulate the body's immune response against specific diseases. Therapeutic enzymes are proteins engineered to replace or enhance certain enzyme functions in the body. Monoclonal antibodies are artificially created antibodies that target specific molecules or cells in the body. Cytokines are proteins that regulate cellular communication in the immune system. Replacement proteins are bioengineered to replace deficient or non-functioning proteins in the body. Peptide antibiotics are synthetic proteins used to combat bacterial infections. Blood products refer to bioengineered proteins derived from human blood that are used for therapeutic purposes.</p></p>
