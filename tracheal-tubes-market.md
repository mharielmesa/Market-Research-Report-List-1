<p><p>Tracheal tubes, including tracheostomy tubes and endotracheal tubes, are medical devices used to provide airway support in different situations. Tracheostomy tubes are inserted into a surgically created opening in the neck to ensure air flow directly into the trachea during cases of severe airway obstruction or for long-term ventilatory support. On the other hand, endotracheal tubes are inserted through the mouth or nose into the trachea during general anesthesia, critical care, or emergency situations to secure the airway and facilitate mechanical ventilation.</p></p>
