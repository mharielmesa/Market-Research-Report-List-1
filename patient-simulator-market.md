<p><p>A patient simulator is a computerized mannequin or device that accurately replicates human anatomical features and physiological responses to simulate realistic patient scenarios. Patient simulators allow healthcare professionals to practice various clinical procedures, critical thinking skills, and communication techniques.</p><p>The market for patient simulators is experiencing significant growth due to several factors. Firstly, the increasing emphasis on patient safety and the need to minimize medical errors in real-life scenarios are driving the adoption of patient simulators. Healthcare organizations are increasingly recognizing the importance of simulation training to improve patient outcomes and reduce costs.</p><p>Additionally, advancements in technology, such as the integration of virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) in patient simulators, are further propelling market growth. VR and AR provide a more immersive and realistic training experience, enabling healthcare professionals to practice complex procedures and develop clinical skills in a virtual setting.</p><p>Furthermore, the COVID-19 pandemic has also boosted the demand for patient simulators as healthcare professionals have faced challenges in accessing real patients due to infection risks and limited resources.
