<p><p>Cervical cancer screening methods include Pap tests, HPV tests, and visual inspection using acetic acid. Pap tests involve collecting cells from the cervix and examining them under a microscope to detect any abnormal changes. HPV tests check for the presence of high-risk types of human papillomavirus, which can increase the risk of cervical cancer. Visual inspection using acetic acid involves applying acetic acid to the cervix and looking for any white areas that may indicate abnormal cell growth. These screening methods help in early detection and prevention of cervical cancer.</p></p>
