<p><p>Neurosurgery Electrosurgical Units (ESUs) are devices used in neurosurgical procedures to cut, coagulate, and seal tissues using electrical energy. The market for ESUs can be categorized into three types - Monopole ESUs, which use one electrode for cutting and coagulation; Bipolar ESUs, which use two electrodes to minimize damage to surrounding tissues; and Vessel Sealing Units, which are specialized ESUs designed for sealing blood vessels during surgery. Each type of ESU serves a specific purpose in neurosurgery and offers distinct benefits to surgeons.</p></p>
