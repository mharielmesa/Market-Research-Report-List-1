<p><strong>The Metagenomic Sequencing Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Shotgun Metagenomic Sequencing</li><li>16S rRNA Sequencing</li><li>Metatranscriptomics</li><li>Whole-genome Sequencing & De Novo Assembly</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Metagenomic sequencing is a technique used to study genetic material from environmental samples. It helps to identify and characterize microorganisms present in the sample. The market for metagenomic sequencing is divided into several types. Shotgun metagenomic sequencing involves sequencing all the DNA present in the sample, providing a comprehensive analysis. 16S rRNA sequencing focuses on a specific gene to identify and compare bacterial species. Metatranscriptomics studies the RNA molecules to understand gene expression in the sample. Whole-genome sequencing analyzes the entire genomic DNA of the microorganisms. De novo assembly involves reconstructing complete genomes from fragmented DNA sequences.</p></p>
