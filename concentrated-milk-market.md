<p><p>Concentrated milk refers to the removal of water from milk, resulting in a thicker consistency and increased shelf life. Condensed milk is prepared by adding sugar to concentrated milk, creating a sweet and viscous product. It is commonly used in baking and dessert recipes. In contrast, evaporated milk is created by removing about 60% of the water from fresh milk, making it creamier but not sweetened. It is often used as a substitute for regular milk in various culinary applications like sauces and soups.</p></p>
