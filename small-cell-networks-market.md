<p><p>Small cell networks refer to the deployment of low-power cellular base stations to enhance wireless connectivity in specific areas. There are various types of small cell networks: </p><p>1. Femtocell: A small base station designed for residential or small office environments to improve indoor coverage and capacity. </p><p>2. Picocell: A slightly larger base station targeting larger indoor environments or smaller outdoor areas. </p><p>3. Microcell: A small base station deployed to cover urban environments, providing improved coverage and capacity in a moderate area. </p><p>4. Metrocell: A larger base station deployed in densely populated urban areas to address high-capacity needs and enhance coverage for a larger area. These small cell networks aim to alleviate network congestion and improve wireless connectivity in specific locations.</p></p>
