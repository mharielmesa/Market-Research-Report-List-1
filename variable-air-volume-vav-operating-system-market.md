<p><ul><li>Single-duct VAV</li><li>Dual-duct VAV</li><li>Induction VAV</li><li>Fan-powered VAV</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Variable Air Volume (VAV) operating systems are used in HVAC systems to control the temperature and airflow in different zones of a building. There are four main types of VAV systems: </p><p>1. Single-duct VAV: It uses a single duct for both supply and return air.</p><p>2. Dual-duct VAV: It uses two separate ducts, one for hot air and another for cold air, offering better control over temperature.</p><p>3. Induction VAV: It works by inducing air into a primary airstream to achieve the desired temperature in each zone.</p><p>4. Fan-powered VAV: It includes a fan to boost air circulation, ensuring proper airflow in each zone.</p></p>
