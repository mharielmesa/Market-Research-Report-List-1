<p><p>Tissue adhesives are used in medical procedures to stick tissues together and promote healing. Protein-based adhesives are made from synthetic or natural proteins, like albumin or gelatin. Collagen-based tissue adhesives use collagen, a protein found in connective tissues. Thrombin-based adhesives consist of thrombin, an enzyme that helps in blood clotting. Fibrin adhesives are derived from fibrinogen and thrombin and form fibrin, which helps seal wounds. Other types of tissue adhesives may include cyanoacrylate or polyethylene glycol-based adhesives. Each type of adhesive offers unique properties and benefits for different medical applications.</p></p>
