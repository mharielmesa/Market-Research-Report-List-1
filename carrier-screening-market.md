<p><ul><li>Molecular Screening Test</li><li>Biochemical Screening Test</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Carrier screening is a method used to identify individuals who carry a gene mutation for certain inherited disorders. It is done prior to or during pregnancy to assess the risk of passing on these disorders to their offspring. There are two main types of carrier screening tests: molecular screening tests and biochemical screening tests. Molecular screening tests analyze DNA for specific gene mutations, while biochemical screening tests analyze blood or urine samples to measure the levels of certain chemicals that may indicate a potential risk for certain disorders.</p></p>
