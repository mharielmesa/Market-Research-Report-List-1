<p><p>Vagus Nerve Stimulators (VNS) are medical devices used to treat various neurological conditions by delivering electrical impulses to the vagus nerve. There are two main types of VNS markets: Direct VNS and Percutaneous VNS. Direct VNS involves surgically implanting a device directly on the vagus nerve, providing continuous stimulation. On the other hand, Percutaneous VNS involves delivering temporary stimulation through a small electrode placed on the neck surface. Both types aim to modulate brain activity and have shown potential in the management of epilepsy and depression.</p></p>
