<p><p>Near Field Communication (NFC) is a technology used for wireless communication between devices in close proximity. In the market, there are three types of NFC: card emulation, reader emulation, and peer-to-peer. In card emulation, a device behaves like a contactless smart card, allowing users to make payments or access secure systems using their smartphones. Reader emulation enables devices to interact with a contactless smart card, like a payment terminal. Peer-to-peer NFC allows two devices to exchange information without the need for a third-party infrastructure, facilitating quick data sharing between devices.</p></p>
