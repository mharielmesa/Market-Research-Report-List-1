<p><p>Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs) are portable medical devices used to deliver an electric shock to treat sudden cardiac arrest. There are two types: semi-automated and fully automated. Semi-automated AEDs require the user to initiate the shock by pressing a button, after analyzing the patient's heart rhythm. They provide voice prompts for guidance during CPR. On the other hand, fully automated AEDs analyze the rhythm and administer the shock automatically, without the need for any user intervention. Both types are designed to be user-friendly and enable quick response in emergency situations.</p></p>
