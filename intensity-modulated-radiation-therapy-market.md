<p><p>Intensity Modulated Radiation Therapy (IMRT) is a type of radiation therapy used to treat cancer. It is a precise and advanced technique that allows for the delivery of high doses of radiation to cancerous cells while minimizing damage to surrounding healthy tissue. Unlike conventional radiation therapy, IMRT uses computer-controlled x-ray beams that are delivered from multiple angles and with varying intensities to conform to the shape of the tumor.</p><p>The global IMRT market is projected to grow at a significant rate during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of cancer, adoption of advanced radiation therapy techniques, technological advancements in radiation therapy devices, and the growing demand for non-invasive treatment options are some of the key factors driving market growth.</p><p>In recent years, there has been a shift towards the use of advanced radiation therapy techniques like IMRT. This is primarily due to its ability to deliver precise doses of radiation, resulting in improved tumor control and reduced side effects. Additionally, the use of IMRT allows for the treatment of complex tumors that are in close proximity to critical organs.</p><p>The market is also witnessing the integration of IMRT with other radiation therapy techniques such as image-guided radiation therapy (IGRT) and stereotactic body radiation therapy (SBRT). This integration enables better targeting and tracking of tumors, further enhancing treatment outcomes.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in radiation therapy devices, such as the introduction of linear accelerators with built-in IMRT capabilities, are expected to boost market growth.
