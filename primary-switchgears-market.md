<p><p>Primary Switchgears are essential components used to control, protect, and isolate electrical circuits in power distribution systems. Gas-insulated switchgears (GIS) employ sulfur hexafluoride (SF6) gas as an insulation medium, offering compactness and high reliability. Vacuum-insulated switchgears (VIS) utilize a vacuum as the insulation medium, providing enhanced safety and durability. Both types of switchgears are crucial in ensuring the efficient and safe functioning of electrical systems by preventing short circuits, overloads, and faults. Their widespread use in industries and utilities highlights their importance in power distribution networks.</p></p>
