<p><p>Genotyping is a process used to determine an individual's genetic makeup. The market for genotyping is dominated by several techniques. Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) is a widely used method that amplifies targeted DNA regions. Sequencing, also known as Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS), involves determining the exact sequence of DNA. Microarray technology allows for the analysis of thousands of genetic markers simultaneously. Electrophoresis separates DNA fragments based on size for analysis. Matrix-Assisted Laser Desorption/Ionization Time-Of-Flight (MALDI-TOF) is a technique used to analyze genetic variation based on mass spectroscopy.</p></p>
