<p><p>Chronic myeloid leukemia (CML) is a type of cancer that affects the blood and bone marrow, leading to the abnormal growth of white blood cells. Over the years, various treatment options have been developed to manage the disease and improve patient outcomes.</p><p>One of the most successful treatments for CML is targeted therapy using tyrosine kinase inhibitors (TKIs). These drugs specifically target the abnormal protein produced by the Philadelphia chromosome, which is responsible for the excessive growth of white blood cells. TKIs, such as imatinib, dasatinib, and nilotinib, have shown significant efficacy in managing CML and have become the standard of care for most patients.</p><p>In some cases, if TKIs fail or are not well-tolerated, alternative treatment options may include stem cell transplant or clinical trials evaluating new therapies. However, these treatments are often reserved for specific situations due to their associated risks and limitations.</p><p>The global chronic myeloid leukemia treatment market has been experiencing steady growth. Factors driving this growth include the increasing prevalence of CML, advancements in technology, and the rising demand for targeted therapies.
