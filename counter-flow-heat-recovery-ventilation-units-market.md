<p><p>Counter-flow heat recovery ventilation units are a type of mechanical ventilation system used in buildings to improve indoor air quality by exchanging heat between the incoming and outgoing air streams. Vertical type units have a vertically oriented core through which the air streams flow, while horizontal type units have a horizontally oriented core. The vertical type is suitable for narrow spaces and high-rise buildings, while the horizontal type is more commonly used in low-rise buildings. Both types increase energy efficiency by recovering and reusing heat, resulting in reduced heating and cooling costs.</p></p>
