<p><p>Humanized mouse models are used in medical research to study human diseases and develop potential treatments. Genetic humanized mouse models are created by introducing specific human genes into mice, allowing scientists to study how these genes function and contribute to disease development. On the other hand, cell-based humanized mouse models involve transplanting human cells or tissues into mice, providing a more complete representation of human physiology and allowing for more accurate drug testing. These models play a crucial role in improving our understanding of diseases and the development of targeted therapies.</p></p>
