<p><p>Residential air purifiers are devices that help improve and maintain the indoor air quality in homes. They work by removing pollutants, such as dust, pet dander, pollen, and other harmful particles, from the air. These purifiers typically consist of filters or other technologies that capture and eliminate these contaminants, ensuring cleaner air for the residents.</p><p>The residential air purifiers market is anticipated to experience significant growth in the coming years. Factors driving this growth include the increasing awareness among individuals about the importance of indoor air quality on health, the rising prevalence of respiratory diseases and allergies, and the growing urbanization and pollution levels in many regions.</p><p>Additionally, the COVID-19 pandemic has brought more attention to the need for clean and healthy indoor air. This has further accelerated the demand for air purifiers, as they can help remove airborne viruses, including the coronavirus.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in technology have led to the development of more efficient and innovative air purifiers. Manufacturers are incorporating features such as smartphone controls, smart sensors, and multiple filtration stages to enhance their performance. These advancements are likely to attract more consumers and drive market growth.</p><p>However, the residential air purifiers market may face challenges regarding affordability and maintenance costs for certain segments of the population. Additionally, the availability of alternative solutions, such as ventilation systems and plants that improve indoor air quality, could pose competition to air purifiers.</p><p>Overall, with the growing concern for indoor air quality and technological advancements, the residential air purifiers market is expected to witness steady growth in the coming years. The projected compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.6% emphasizes the positive outlook for the market.</p></p>

Ion and ozone generator air purifiers release ions or ozone to neutralize pollutants. Other types include photocatalytic oxidation, UV germicidal lamps, and heat sterilizers.
